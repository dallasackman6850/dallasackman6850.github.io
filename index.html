What is a quote

Posted on 18. Mar, 2021 by Admin in FAQ, with Comments 18

What is a quote - concept, features, scope.

The term “quote” comes from the French verb coter, which literally means “to measure”. In the future, the term began to be interpreted in the context of the expression financial quote, which means "financial quotation", and began to be widely used in the second half of the 1700s, when various types of securities appeared on the European market. Gradually, the conceptual base expanded, and today it includes not only stocks or bonds, but also currency, valuable materials and goods.

What are quotes on the securities market for?

Posted on 3. Mar, 2021 by Sara in FAQ, with Comments 29

The following financial instruments are mainly used on the exchanges.

Securities - stocks, bonds, shares, etc.

Commodities - oil, precious metals, agricultural raw materials, etc.

Each of the designated categories has its own quoting characteristics. For example, stocks are valued at once by two indicators - buy and sell prices. The value of bonds is determined as a percentage of their par value. Moreover, all quotes are quoted in national currency.
